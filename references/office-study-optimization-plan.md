# Office / Study Optimization Plan

This plan adapts `spec-evo` for everyday office and learning scenarios.

## Design Goal

Make AI assistance feel:
- trustworthy enough to use
- lightweight enough to keep using
- structured enough to reduce rework

## Optimization 1 — Shift from autonomy to bounded assistive loops

For office/study users, default loops should be:
- capture
- summarize
- verify
- act

Not:
- decide
- execute broadly
- remember forever

## Optimization 2 — Treat summary quality as a first-class validation problem

Many mainstream AI tasks are summaries in disguise:
- meeting notes
- lecture notes
- reading summaries
- email recaps
- spreadsheet takeaways

So validation should check:
- did it miss key actions?
- did it invent facts?
- did it preserve tone and intent?
- is it short enough to be useful?

## Optimization 3 — Separate “help me finish” from “help me learn”

Office workflow often wants speed.
Study workflow needs retention and understanding.

So the system should distinguish:
- completion-oriented outputs
- comprehension-oriented outputs

Examples:
- office: draft follow-up email from meeting notes
- study: turn reading into quiz questions and self-explanation prompts

## Optimization 4 — Promote reviewable templates before automation

For mainstream users, the best first materializations are often:
- meeting summary template
- action-item checklist
- study recap template
- email review checklist

Scripts and skills come later.

## Optimization 5 — Design for visible wins in one day

A mainstream user should feel value through:
- fewer missed action items
- less rewriting of emails
- easier review of meetings/classes
- better study recall

If the system does not create visible relief fast, adoption dies.

## Recommended New Starter Packs

### A. Meeting-to-Action Pack
For office workers drowning in meetings.

### B. Inbox-to-Draft Pack
For users overwhelmed by email drafting and follow-up.

### C. Lecture-to-Recall Pack
For students who rely on summaries but need better retention.

### D. Reading-to-Notes Pack
For users processing articles, PDFs, or class readings.

## Suggested Validation Priorities

1. summary faithfulness
2. action-item completeness
3. tone / audience fit
4. source grounding
5. active-recall effectiveness for study workflows

## Mainstream Rule

Do not ask ordinary users to think in terms of capability taxonomies first.

Give them a starter flow, a review checklist, and one measurable win.
