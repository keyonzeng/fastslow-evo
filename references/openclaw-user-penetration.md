# OpenClaw User Penetration

This file is about adoption strategy, not ability definition.

## Why OpenClaw is the best first proving ground

OpenClaw users are more likely than generic AI users to:
- interact with the same agent repeatedly
- care about continuity, memory, and behavior drift
- notice and correct delivery, tone, and workflow mistakes
- use agents across chat, tools, documents, and recurring tasks

That creates dense feedback loops, which makes FastSlow Evo easier to validate.

## First-wave adoption targets

### 1. Power users

Traits:
- persistent use over weeks, not one-off prompting
- strong preference signals
- willingness to refine behavior

Why they matter:
- they generate high-quality correction signals
- they benefit quickly from fast-loop improvements
- they expose whether slow-loop promotion is actually useful

### 2. Builders and workflow designers

Traits:
- repeated tasks
- visible failure modes
- comfort with scripts, templates, and workflow rules

Why they matter:
- they create recurrence quickly
- they can tell whether a tiny fix should become a durable asset

## Second-wave targets

### 3. Heavy users in Claude Code / Cursor / OpenCode

They can adopt the method and templates, but they are not the primary runtime context.

## Third-wave targets

### 4. Office and study users

They should consume simplified packs and visible wins, not the full mechanism first.

## Penetration Rule

Prove FastSlow Evo with users who generate dense, high-quality feedback before optimizing for broad low-ceremony adoption.
