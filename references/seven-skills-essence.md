# The Essence of the Seven Skills

This note captures the deepest useful idea from the earlier seven-skill comparison.

## 1. self-improving-agent
Essence:
Low-friction experience capture with clear promotion targets.

What to learn:
- make recording easy
- make promotion targets obvious
- never let useful corrections evaporate

## 2. reflect / specweave reflect
Essence:
Extract learning signals from real corrections and approvals.

What to learn:
- not every line matters
- signal extraction matters more than raw volume
- central memory can compound learning

## 3. hindsight-local
Essence:
Task-after reflection turns hindsight into reusable improvement.

What to learn:
- many useful patterns are only visible after completion
- hindsight is a rich source of tiny fixes

## 4. memory-reflect
Essence:
Reflection should not die in-session; it should feed memory.

What to learn:
- reflection and retention should reinforce each other

## 5. memory
Essence:
Long-term continuity is a prerequisite for durable improvement.

What to learn:
- progress is fragile without stable retrieval

## 6. keep
Essence:
Useful knowledge needs durable, retrievable, low-friction storage.

What to learn:
- storage quality determines whether learning survives

## 7. capability-evolver
Essence:
Evolution needs governance, protocol, and rollback awareness.

What to learn:
- promotion is a high-stakes act
- auditable evolution is stronger than vague self-modification

## What FastSlow Evo should do better

FastSlow Evo should surpass them by owning the missing layer:

- not just capture
- not just reflect
- not just store
- not just evolve

It should decide:
- what deserves fast local adaptation
- what deserves slow durable promotion
- what should stay under observation
- what should be ignored as noise
